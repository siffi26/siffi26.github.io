Siffi Singh

I am an Applied Scientist at Amazon Web Services (AWS), where I help teach machines to understand and reason over human conversations. I work with the Amazon Transcribe and Amazon Connect teams, building multilingual conversational AI systems for speech understanding and analytics. My work focuses on real-time speech translation, privacy-preserving PII redaction, semantic understanding of conversations, and generative AI pipelines that discover trends and insights across large volumes of conversations.

Previously at Amazon, I worked on the Alexa Natural Language Understanding and Alexa Information teams, contributing to systems for semantic retrieval, structured knowledge extraction, and pipelines that automatically build knowledge graphs from information on the web.

I also collaborate with Prof. Sergei Gukov at California Institute of Technology (Department of Mathematics), and previously collaborated with Prof. Burigede Liu at the University of Cambridge (Department of Engineering).

I received my Master of Science in Electrical and Computer Science Engineering from National Chiao Tung University, Taiwan, where I worked with Prof. Hang Hsueh-Ming on deep learning approaches for face recognition and clustering. Earlier, I completed my Bachelor of Technology in Computer Science with distinction from Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam Technical University, India.

Before graduate school, I worked on computer vision and autonomous systems research at the Industrial Technology Research Institute (ITRI) in Taiwan, developing vision components for autonomous driving systems. I also collaborated with Prof. Carlsson C. Fung on applying machine learning techniques to radar sensor data for autonomous vehicles.

I enjoy collaborating with curious teams and leading projects where new ideas emerge through exploration, rigorous thinking, and shared curiosity.
